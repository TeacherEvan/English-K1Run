# English K1 Run Task Notes

## Design Context

_This section is inferred from the current codebase, repository documentation, and existing UI patterns. Update it when direct stakeholder preferences become available._

### Users

English K1 Run is primarily used by kindergarten and early-primary learners in teacher-guided classroom settings, most often on tablets, touch displays, and shared classroom browsers. Students need large, obvious interaction targets and instant feedback; teachers need a menu and settings flow that is quick to launch, easy to supervise, and reliable under classroom pressure. The interface should help children practice vocabulary, recognition, and listening in short, repeatable rounds without reading-heavy friction.

### Brand Personality

Playful, encouraging, warm.

The product should feel optimistic and reassuring rather than chaotic or hyper-competitive. It should create delight, confidence, and a sense of “I can do this,” with rewards that feel celebratory but never overwhelming. Because the app supports multiple classroom languages, the tone should stay visually universal, friendly, and welcoming across cultures.

### Aesthetic Direction

Lean into a touch-first, nature-backed, toy-like classroom aesthetic with polished game energy. Keep the turtle mascot, large tactile controls, friendly rounded forms, and bright kid-facing colors, but refine them into a more intentional visual system. Favor light-first presentation for classrooms, with dark mode as support rather than the default mood. Use sky and ocean blues, leaf greens, warm trophy golds, and softly tinted neutrals that feel alive instead of generic gray. Nature photography and animal or emoji content are part of the product DNA, so decorative choices should feel outdoorsy, curious, and alive—not corporate, futuristic, or sterile.

The memorable signature should be a warm nature-meets-playroom world: real outdoor backdrops, a cheerful turtle mascot, and oversized tactile controls that feel made for small hands.

Reference cues already present in the product:

- Touch-first gameplay on large surfaces
- Nature and animal imagery
- Playful motion and celebratory feedback
- Multilingual classroom use
- High readability at distance

Anti-direction:

- No generic SaaS dashboard styling
- No dark neon gamer aesthetic
- No overused glassmorphism or card-on-card clutter
- No ad-like mobile game chaos
- No tiny controls, thin contrast, or reading-heavy layouts

### Design Principles

1. **Touch-first clarity wins.** Prioritize big targets, instant comprehension, and strong hierarchy over decorative complexity.
2. **Reward with purpose.** Motion, color, and celebration should confirm success and build confidence, not create noise.
3. **Warmth over slickness.** Choose visual details that feel friendly, natural, and classroom-safe rather than trendy or techy.
4. **Design for shared spaces.** Interfaces must remain readable from a distance, fast to operate by teachers, and calm under repetition.
5. **Accessibility is part of the aesthetic.** High contrast, reduced-motion respect, multilingual flexibility, and clear focus states are core design features, not afterthoughts.
